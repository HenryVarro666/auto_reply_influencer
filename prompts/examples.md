# Good-comment examples (few-shot reference)

These are the gold-standard examples the generator learns the voice from.
Two groups:

- **Group A — real comments we actually posted** (screenshots in `example/*.jpg`).
  They were on *education* posts, so the college angle was natural. NOTE: they
  include a link — **we no longer use links** (brand mention only). Copy the
  *helpful, conversational, recommend-a-tool* tone, NOT the URL.
- **Group B — football-themed promo lines** (from `example/Promo Posts.docx`).
  These show how to bridge a football idea to a college-application benefit.
  This is the key move when the post is about football, not school.

---

## Group A — real posted comments (tone to copy; drop the link)

> For those high school students who want to get into these universities, I suggest this new EZCollegeApp to streamline the college application.

> For those high schoolers who want to get into their dream colleges, I recommend this new tool, EZCollegeApp, to streamline their application.

Why they work: they open by speaking to a relevant slice of the audience
("for those who want to…"), then *recommend* rather than *sell*, naming
EZCollegeApp once as a helpful tool. Calm, peer-to-peer, no hype.

## Style rules distilled from the examples

1. **One leading emoji**, chosen to fit the post (⚽🏆🎯🧤🥅🔥 etc.).
2. **React to THIS post first** — name the player/club/result you actually see in
   the image/caption, like a real fan, before any bridge.
3. **Bridge a football idea → a college-application benefit** matched to the
   account type (player → training, club/national team → squad/teamwork,
   league/tournament → rounds/bracket, manager → coach, legend/retired → "the
   greats started somewhere").
4. **Mention EZCollegeApp once**, as a helpful AI college counselor — recommend,
   do not sell. No pricing, no "DM me", no "click here".
5. **No link / no URL.** Ever.
6. **No contractions** ("do not", "does not", "you are").
7. Short: 1–2 sentences, ~≤160 characters. At most 2 hashtags, the last
   being `#EZCollegeApp`.
8. Must read as a genuine comment a fan could leave — if it reads like an ad
   drop, it is wrong.
